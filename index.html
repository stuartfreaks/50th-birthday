Stuart's 50th Birthday

Monforte d'Alba, Piemonte

Welcome to a long weekend of Barolo, fine food and good company in one of Italy's most beautiful wine regions.

Explore the Weekend

Accommodation

I Colli Rossi

We will all be staying at the excellent I Colli Rossi guest house. Set in six hectares of Barolo-producing vineyards just underneath the hill town of Monforte d'Alba, it is run by the ever-charming Bianca Giaccone.

Bianca provides a breakfast of local products every morning, along with a tour of the wine-making facilities and a tasting of all the I Colli Rossi wines.

The town is just 11 minutes on foot and has a number of bars and restaurants, as well as a weekly market selling local meat and cheese.

The bedrooms are spacious doubles with large separate bathrooms and a small balcony offering views across the whole of the Langhe, with the arc of the Alps stretching along the horizon on a clear day.

Eating & Drinking

The Main Lunch Celebration – Saturday

La Coccinella
Serravalle Langhe

www.trattoriacoccinella.it

A charming little Trattoria in the beautiful town of Serravalle offering fine food and excellent service in a convivial family atmosphere.

If the weather permits, a small digestive stroll around the town afterwards is sure to provide views of the Alps and surrounding countryside.

The young and energetic will be afforded the opportunity to burn off some fat, wild and free, in this traffic-free hamlet.

Friday – Trip to Barbaresco

For those who fancy it, a walk around the town of Barbaresco followed by lunch at the excellent Osteria Campamac.

Osteria Campamac

There will also be an opportunity to climb the famous Tower of Barbaresco, a medieval watchtower constructed in the 14th Century by the Visconti Family. The views are quite good too.

The Rest of the Weekend

The programme is largely up to where the mood takes you.

The general plan is to enjoy a generous lunch most days and make do with a lighter evening meal in Monforte d'Alba.

Those wishing to sample the delights of any of the Michelin-starred restaurants in the vicinity will, of course, be more than welcome.

Wine, Vineyards & Recovery

The guest house is set in six hectares of Nebbiolo vines in the shadow of Monforte d'Alba. There is a garden where you can sit outside and there is a winery where they make excellent Barolo and Nebbiolo wines.

Bianca will give us a tour of the wine-making facilities and a wine tasting for anyone interested in learning more about I Colli Rossi wines.

Breakfast at the guest house is included and is perhaps the largest all-inclusive breakfast I have witnessed in my entire life.

Early risers should find plenty to keep them going until a 1:30 lunch engagement.

What If I Don't Just Want To Sit About Eating and Drinking Wine All The Time?

A reasonable question.

Children and adults looking for more than a steeping in some of the planet's finest red wines will find plenty to do.

There are castles open to visitors, the Toy Museum in Bra, and the Museum of Magic in Cherasco.

There are also walks, treks and countless little towns dotted across the hillsides waiting to be explored.

It's too early for skiing, but there may be a dusting of snow on the mountains.

Getting There & Away

For those travelling from Scotland, there are currently two daily flights from Glasgow Prestwick to Turin Airport.

Turin Airport is approximately 1 hour and 15 minutes by car from I Colli Rossi.

Turin Airport

I Colli Rossi

Car hire is available at the airport and is generally the easiest way to reach Monforte d'Alba.

For those already in Italy, Monforte d'Alba is approximately a two-hour drive from Milan.
